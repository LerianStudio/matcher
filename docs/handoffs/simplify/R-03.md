## Why I considered Option 2 relocation and rejected it

Per T-016's precedent (interface relocation when there's a single bounded-context consumer), I considered:

- **Move `BridgeOrchestrator` interface from `shared/ports/bridge_orchestrator.go` → `discovery/ports/`.** Both consumer (worker) and implementer (orchestrator) live in discovery. The adapter doesn't cross any boundary. Net: ~95 LOC saved in shared/ports, ~95 LOC added in discovery/ports. **Wash.** The shared/ports placement was already the path of least friction (worker tests stay structurally satisfied; nothing reaches into shared/ports.mocks since T-004 cleaned those orphan mocks). Skipped.
- **Move `BridgeSourceResolver` + `BridgeSourceTarget` → `discovery/services/command/`.** Local interface, structurally satisfied by the cross-adapter. Net: ~30 LOC saved in shared/ports. But `ErrBridgeSourceUnresolvable` sentinel must stay in shared/ports (the adapter returns it, and shared/adapters/cross can't import discovery without breaking layering). Result: split of related types across packages — worse coherence than the current placement. Skipped.
- **Inline `ExtractionLifecycleLinkWriterAdapter` onto `*BridgeExtractionOrchestrator`.** Possible because the adapter doesn't actually cross a boundary. But the chaos test (`tests/chaos/extraction_lifecycle_link_chaos_test.go`) imports the adapter directly to inject Toxiproxy latency at the link seam. Inlining forces the chaos test to either (a) drive the full bridge orchestrator with all sub-deps wired, or (b) reach into a private orchestrator method via a test helper. Both make the chaos surface noisier. Skipped.

In all three cases, the burden-of-proof rubric (cost of change > LOC won, or work unit changes nothing measurable) failed.
